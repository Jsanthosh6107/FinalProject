Death

The end of acute radiation syndrome

Content

Death typically occurs as a result of sepsis and infections from immunosuppression, hemorrhagic shock due to loss of platelets, or organ failure from cumulative radiation damage.

[Speaker]: There is no medical intervention capable of reversing the inevitable outcome. The damage to the body's cellular structure, particularly in the bone marrow, gastrointestinal tract, and central nervous system, is so severe that critical systems shut down irreversibly. bone marrow leaves the body unable to create blood cells leading to fatal infections and hemorrhage.

The longest survivor of ARS was Hisashi Ouchi. His body was ravaged on a cellular level, leaving his DNA irreparably damaged and his immune system nonexistent. Doctors decided to attempt to save his life.

Ouchi was kept alive for 83 excruciating days, potentially against his will. His skin peeled away, his organs failed, and he endured unrelenting pain as his body literally disintegrated while he was conscious.

If a loved one has suffered from ARS, please reach out to us, and we will add them to the list.
